Diagnostic Evolution

From 1990 ACR tender points to 2016 criteria focusing on widespread pain index (WPI) and symptom severity scale (SSS), now classified under ICD-11 chronic primary pain.

Oxidative Stress

Elevated MDA, AOPP, and reduced antioxidant capacity (TAC, GSH, SOD, GPx) indicate profound cellular redox imbalance as a core pathophysiological mechanism.

‚ö°

Mitochondrial Dysfunction

Impaired Complex I activity, reduced ATP synthesis, and cellular energy deficits contribute to fatigue and exercise intolerance characteristic of FM.

Pathophysiology & Small Fiber Pathology

Multiple interconnected mechanisms underlying FM symptoms

Small Fiber Neuropathy

40-50% of FM patients show reduced intraepidermal nerve fiber density (IENFD) on skin biopsy, providing objective structural pathology.

Central Sensitization

Amplification of pain signals in the CNS, aberrant pain processing, and neuroinflammation with glial activation.

Temporal Dynamics

Immediate Phase

0-30 minutes

Ozone-derived LOPs (4-HNE) covalently modify Keap1 cysteine residues, disrupting Keap1-Nrf2 interaction. MAPK activation phosphorylates Nrf2, facilitating nuclear translocation.

Acute Phase

Minutes to 6 hours

Stabilized Nrf2 accumulates and translocates to nucleus, heterodimerizing with Maf proteins and binding to AREs. Transcriptional activation of Nrf2 gene battery begins.

Adaptive Phase

6-72 hours

Peak expression of antioxidant enzymes (5-10 fold increases). Maximum cellular stress resistance and anti-inflammatory capacity. Redox homeostasis restoration.

Resolution Phase

3-14 days

Nrf2 returns to baseline but cytoprotective proteins persist due to long half-lives. Enhanced stress resistance maintained, providing basis for inter-treatment intervals.

Pre-Treatment Assessment

‚ñº

Patient Phenotyping:

  • High oxidative stress (MDA >2.5 Œºmol/L)
  • Inflammatory phenotype (hsCRP >3 mg/L)
  • Mitochondrial dysfunction (fatigue, exercise intolerance)
  • Small fiber pathology (burning pain, dysesthesias)

Mandatory Screening:

  • G6PD Activity - ABSOLUTE REQUIREMENT (deficiency = contraindication)
  • Complete blood count (CBC)
  • Comprehensive metabolic panel (CMP)
  • Thyroid function (TSH, free T4)

Baseline Biomarkers:

  • Oxidative stress: MDA, AOPP, 8-OHdG
  • Antioxidant capacity: TAC, SOD, GPx
  • Inflammation: hsCRP, IL-6, TNF-Œ±
  • Glutathione status: GSH, GSH:GSSG ratio

Intensive Phase (4-6 weeks)

‚ñº

Core Parameters:

  • Frequency: 2-3 sessions/week (spaced 2-4 days apart)
  • Duration: 4-6 weeks total
  • Total sessions: 8-18 (based on severity)
  • Blood volume: 100-150 mL (adjust for patient size)
  • Reinfusion rate: 1 drop/second, max 90 drops/minute

Ozone Concentration (Progressive Escalation):

  • Initial sessions (1-3): 10-20 Œºg/mL
  • Middle sessions (4-8): 20-30 Œºg/mL
  • Later sessions (9-15): 30-40 Œºg/mL
  • Maximum: 40 Œºg/mL (hormetic zone ceiling)

Interim Assessments:

  • Week 2-3: Pain/function, tolerance check
  • Week 4-5: Comprehensive reassessment + PGIC
  • Week 6: Full outcome + biomarker repeat

Safety & Contraindications

Ensuring patient safety through proper screening and monitoring

‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL: Mandatory G6PD Screening

Glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase (G6PD) deficiency is an ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATION to ozone therapy. This genetic condition affects ~400 million people globally with highest prevalence in Mediterranean, African, Asian, and Middle Eastern populations. G6PD screening MUST be performed before any treatment‚Äîdeficiency can cause life-threatening hemolytic crisis.

üõë Absolute Contraindications

  • G6PD deficiency / Favism
  • Acute hemolytic anemia
  • Active bleeding / coagulopathies
  • Severe thrombocytopenia (<50k/ŒºL)
  • Active hyperthyroidism
  • Pregnancy
  • Recent MI (<3 months)
  • Patent foramen ovale

‚ö° Relative Contraindications

  • Uncontrolled hypertension (BP >180/110)
  • Active anticoagulation therapy
  • Significant anemia (Hb <10 g/dL)
  • Active malignancy (consult oncologist)
  • Neurodegenerative disorders (consult neurologist)
  • Seizure disorder (theoretical concern)
